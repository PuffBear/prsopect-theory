# **Literature Review v2: Novelty-Kill Assessment for MARL Centralization and Behavioral Tipping Points**

## **0\. The Question Under Review (Frozen Before Searching)**

The foundational premise of this comprehensive literature review is to rigorously stress-test three nested framings of a specific research question against the existing body of knowledge in multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL), evolutionary game theory (EGT), and complex systems. The objective is to identify the narrowest, most defensible claim by actively seeking out prior work that preempts the proposed theoretical or empirical contributions. The analysis evaluates the following nested levels of inquiry:

* **L1 (Defend against, do not claim):** Does reward centralization ![][image1] induce a collapse$\\leftrightarrow$coordination phase transition in cooperative MARL? This level assesses whether the fundamental structural dynamic of multi-agent credit assignment—specifically the transition from shared team rewards to localized individual rewards—results in a quantifiable systemic collapse.  
* **L2 (The defensible spine):** Do agent-level behavioral biases (specifically, prospect-theoretic loss aversion ![][image2]) reshape the centralization-induced collapse boundary? This framing asks whether coordination collapse is a property of the reward structure alone, or a complex interaction between the reward structure and the non-linear utility curvature of the agents experiencing it.  
* **L3 (The moonshot):** Can a minority of behaviorally-biased agents shift a system-level coordination phase boundary, constituting a behavioral collapse mechanism distinct from the structural one? This hypothesis explores whether a heterogeneous sub-population operating under different cognitive parameters can drag a broader risk-neutral MARL ecosystem into an inefficient, zero-sum attractor basin.

**Pre-commitment:** This review is designed to search for evidence that these hypotheses have already been resolved, not to find tangential support for a preferred narrative. Kills are recorded precisely to dictate the final research trajectory, ensuring the resulting methodology occupies a genuinely open space in the literature.
